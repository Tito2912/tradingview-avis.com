The “best” tool is the one that lets you run a clean workflow with minimal friction.

<h2 id="when">When to consider switching</h2>

You want an alternative if:

- you need specific **execution** features,
- you want deeper **automation**,
- you have strong **market data** constraints,
- your total cost (plan + data) no longer makes sense.

Otherwise, optimizing your TradingView setup is often the fastest win.

<h2 id="criteria">Comparison criteria</h2>

Use 5 simple axes:

1. **Charting**: readability, templates, multi-timeframes.
2. **Alerts**: server-side, conditions, webhooks.
3. **Automation**: scripting, testing, integrations.
4. **Brokers**: country/instrument compatibility, order types.
5. **Total cost**: plan + data + add-ons.

<h2 id="options">Common option families</h2>

Instead of a long list, group tools by intention:

- charting/community-first tools,
- broker-centric execution tools,
- advanced analysis/data tools,
- niche platforms (crypto, futures, options).

Keep your shortlist to 2–3 tools, otherwise you won’t test anything.

<h2 id="decision">The 7-day decision rule</h2>

Minimal test over 7 days:

- one watchlist,
- one setup,
- three alerts,
- one execution scenario.

If it fails the test, you save time and move on.
